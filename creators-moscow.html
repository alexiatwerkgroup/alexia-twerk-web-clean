The Moscow heels school.

Russia invented the modern heels-choreo language. Лада Гоцци at Moscow Dance Centre NRG turned it into a curriculum. Eight creators to watch — six studio + two solo — and the 2.3M combined views that proved the method works.

#1 · MDC NRG

Лада Гоцци

Moscow Dance Centre NRG's lead high-heels instructor. Pharaoh "Пломбир" sits at 167K views + 3,360 likes — the most-replayed cut across our main archive. Pure technique, zero gimmicks.

Profile → #2 · TWERKIT Studio

Abasheva Yana

Rihanna "Needed Me" choreo hits 904K views. Yana founded TWERKIT Studio — the beginner pipeline that launched half the Russian scene. Workshops every 6 weeks.

Profile → #3 · Independent

Kate Knaub

Goody "Брокколи" — 139K views. Kate works between studios, not locked to one brand. Her style is looser than Лада's but sharper than pure street freestyle.

Profile → #4 · Ufa rooftop

Dasha Kolesnikova

Nicki Minaj "Yikes" on a Ufa rooftop. 136K views. Proves you don't need a fancy studio — just sharp execution and a good camera operator (her partner).

Profile → #5 · Volzhsky

Школа Танцев Street Project

From Volzhsky (not Moscow proper but the scene). "TWERK Shake Dat" is their most-replayed cut. Studio has a Jazz-funk Kids crossover program that's remarkable.

Profile → #6 · Indica team

Lena Indica

Indica team does workshops across Russia + the EU. Heels choreo specialist. Pop Smoke "Dior" sits at 303K views — the crossover track that converted half the K-dance audience.

Profile → #7 · Moscow · solo

Sugaarrbbaby

The cleanest solo bedroom-frame creator on the Moscow scene. MDC NRG-adjacent training, self-directed practice, slow-build phrasing. The counterweight to studio ensemble work.

Profile → #8 · Odessa · solo (UA)

Nika Chilli (Odessa)

Ukrainian solo dancer based in Odessa. Black Sea coastal scene. Long-take street fancam. Not Nika Chill (LA) — different person, same archive.

Why Moscow?

Russia's dance-school network is older and more structured than almost anywhere else. Technique is rigorous. Heels work gets rehearsed until the leg line is textbook. The result: a scene that looks different from the American street lineage and different from K-dance — it's its own school. Every major heels-choreo channel in 2026 traces back to MDC NRG or one of its graduates.
