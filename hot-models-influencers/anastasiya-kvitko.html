The short version

Anastasiya Kvitko is one of the original Instagram-era curve models — Donetsk-born, LA-based, and one of the rare creators in this lane to build a real product business on top of the audience. The brand has two layers: the personal one (curve modeling, swimwear and bodycon campaigns, sustained 12M+ Instagram following) and the entrepreneurial one (founder of Eve Swimwear, her own brand, which is the part of the career that stops the audience from being a pure social-media bet).

Why she's on this roster

Three reasons. First, the curve-model search ecosystem is one of the densest categories on this side of the internet, and Kvitko is one of the names that anchors it — search demand around her stays remarkably consistent year over year. Second, she's the cleanest example we have of how a curve creator transitions from "model with a following" into "founder with a model arm" — directly relevant to anyone reading our blog content on the creator economy. Third, her Russian-language audience overlaps directly with our existing Russian-language SEO footprint.

Career arc (what we can verify publicly)

2012
Moves to Miami at 17. Starts modeling work in the U.S.
2015
International press coverage starts treating her as a recognizable name. The "Russian Kim K." comparison is widespread (and one she's publicly pushed back on).
2017
Audience growth on Instagram crosses into the millions. Travel-photography and swimwear campaigns become the dominant content cycles.
2018–2019
Launches Eve Swimwear, her own swimwear brand. The career transitions from pure modeling to model-plus-founder.
2020–2023
Sustained Instagram growth past 12M. Recurring press cycles around new shoots, brand campaigns, and product launches.
2024–2026
Active across IG, X, and TikTok. Eve Swimwear remains the operational core; the modeling brand is the audience-aggregation layer.

How she compares to Twerkhub talent

Kvitko is the rare model-as-founder case on this roster. Most of the names we feature operate inside the standard funnel — content first, monetization endpoint second. Kvitko added a third layer: a product business that compounds independently of the social-media reach. It's a useful counter-reference for anyone reading our platform-comparison breakdown — proof that the OnlyFans endpoint is one good option but not the only one.
