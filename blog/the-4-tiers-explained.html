The 4 tiers, explained.

By Anti · firestarterMar 14, 20268 min read

Four tiers. Two ways to reach each: pay the subscription or earn the tokens. Here's what every tier actually unlocks, with no marketing fluff.

Tier 1 · Basic

Basic

$0 · 0-499 tokens

What you get: Full access to all 7 playlist pages. Every hot-ranking top-5 video watchable. Previews for every archive video. Blog access. Glossary. Search. Community forum (read-only).

What you don't get: Full archive grid beyond top-5. Discord lounge. Custom content. Early drops.

Who it's for: Anyone arriving. Build token habit, see if the platform fits. Zero commitment.

Tier 2 · Medium

Medium

$9.99/mo · 500-1,999 tokens

What you get: Everything Basic plus: Discord lounge access (members #general, #drops, #requests channels). 12-hour preview window on Thursday drops. Ability to post in forum + Discord.

What you don't get: Full archive grid. Custom content. 48h early access.

Who it's for: Active watchers ready to participate in the community. Active Medium members on average hit Medium threshold within 4-5 days of registering.

Tier 3 · Premium

Premium

$29.99/mo · 2,000-9,999 tokens

What you get: Everything Medium plus: full archive grid — 1,500+ videos, all 275 in /playlist/, all 60 in each niche playlist. 48h early access on Thursday drops. Priority support (24h response). Exclusive Premium-only monthly clips.

What you don't get: Custom content. 72h early access. Direct Alexia Discord.

Who it's for: Serious watchers. This is where 60% of total watch-time happens. Most people stay at Premium long-term.

Tier 4 · VIP Top

VIP Top

$99.99/mo · 10,000+ tokens

What you get: Everything Premium plus: custom content requests (Alexia and available creators will prioritize your request). Direct Discord line to Alexia — not a support channel, her actual DMs. 72h early access on all drops. Monthly VIP-only exclusive clip that never enters any public playlist. Name in the members roster on the community page.

Who it's for: Highest-commitment members. Currently 126 VIP Top members. Average tenure 8+ months.

Two paths to each tier.

Token-earned and subscription-paid tiers unlock identical content. The difference is the commitment contract.

  • Subscription path: instant access, cancel anytime, standard consumer relationship. If you stop paying, tier drops next billing cycle.
  • Token path: earn your way up via daily activity — exploring, watching, finishing clips, referrals, login streak. Tier stays as long as you stay within the band. Drop below a threshold and your tier adjusts.

How long does each path take?

Empirical data from 2025 cohort (N=412 new members).

  • Basic → Medium: 4.2 days median on active engagement (~100 tokens/day).
  • Medium → Premium: 11.5 days additional (~140 tokens/day with streak bonus).
  • Premium → VIP Top: 16 days additional (~500 tokens/day with referrals).
  • Full Basic → VIP Top: ~31 days for consistent daily members. See the complete 21-day plan.

What happens if you fall behind on tokens?

Your tier softens over 30 days. Example: you were VIP Top at 10,500 tokens, then you stop logging in for 30 days. Tokens don't expire, but the streak multipliers lapse. You drop to Premium tier access. Your tokens stay — next time you engage, you're back in the bracket.

There's a 30-day grace period on subscriptions too. If your card fails, tier stays active for 30 days while we try to re-charge. After that it reverts to your token-earned level.

Can you mix?

Yes. Many members subscribe at Medium and earn up to VIP Top via tokens. The tier that applies is whichever is higher at any given moment. If you're paying $9.99 Medium but earned 12,000 tokens, you get VIP Top access.

Why we built it this way.

Simple subscription models (OnlyFans, Patreon) reward willingness to pay. Token economies reward attention paid. Both are valid signals. Combining them means a dancer fresh out of college without disposable income still has a path to VIP Top. A busy professional without time to log in daily can still access it instantly via subscription. We don't want to exclude either group.
